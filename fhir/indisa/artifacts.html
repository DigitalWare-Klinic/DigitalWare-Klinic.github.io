This page provides a list of the FHIR artifacts defined as part of this implementation guide.

Structures: Resource Profiles

These define constraints on FHIR resources for systems conforming to this implementation guide

Account

Patient account, where all charges made at the outpatient or hospital level are found.
Appointment

Registered appointments of the patient for each general and specialized medical consultation and the performance of laboratory and imaging tests, among others.
Claim

A provider issued list of professional services and products which have been provided, or are to be provided, to a patient.
Document Electronic

Electronic composition document that allows the exchange of information between processes or between integration points and that has to do with the patient’s medical information.
Condition

A clinical condition, problem, diagnosis, or other event, situation, issue, or clinical concept that has risen to a level of concern.
Coverage patient

Patient’s health coverage policy.
Device

A type of a manufactured item that is used in the provision of healthcare without being substantially changed through that activity.
Diagnostic report for laboratory

Clinical diagnostic reports of the patient (laboratory).
Diagnostic report for imaging study

Clinical diagnostic reports of the patient (imaging study and cardiovascular tests).
Encounter Klinic

Clinical and administrative encounter of the patient (ambulatory or inpatient), during the provision of health services.
Endpoint

Contact points where the exchange, update and consultation of medical information of the patient is allowed.
Healthcare Service

health services that are provided to the patient, once they enter the provider’s facilities.
Imaging Study

A set of images produced in single study at patient.
Device

FHIR resource profile for Active Implantable Medical Devices (AIMD), invasive medical devices, custom medical devices, and invasive or reusable surgical medical devices.

Many of these devices are partially or completely absorbed.

Such as: pacemaker, extends, catheter, IUD, and surgical instruments.
Location Area

Physical location of each of the clinical and administrative areas within a building: Floor, Area, Wing, Waiting room, intensive care unit, among others.
Location Bed

Physical location of each of the clinical areas within a building: Bed.
Location Building

Physical location of each of the clinical and administrative areas within a building: Building, Wing of building, Tower, etc.
Location Functional Unit

Physical location of each of the clinical and administrative areas within a building: Functional Unit.
Location Room

Physical location of each of the clinical and administrative areas within a building: Room, Consulting room, etc.
Medication Administration

Describes the event of a patient consuming or otherwise being administered a medication.
Medication dispense

Indicates that a medication product is to be or has been dispensed for a named person/patient. This includes a description of the medication product (supply) provided and the instructions for administering the medication. The medication dispense is the result of a pharmacy system responding to a medication order.
Medication

Identification and definition of a medication for the purposes of prescribing, dispensing, and administering a medication as well as for making statements about medication use.
Medication Request

An order or request for both supply of the medication and the instructions for administration of the medication to a patient.
Message header

Message header in the messaging exchange.
Observation

Simple measurements and statements made about a patient. (Vital signs, laboratory results, imaging results, clinical findings, measurements made with medical devices, etc).
Organization Healthcare Provider

Organization Healthcare Provider.
Patient

Data of patient.
Practitioner

Practitioner of healht.
Practitioner Role

Role of practitioner.
Procedure

Medical procedures in health.
Questionnaire response

Questionnaire responses
Questionnaire

Questionnaire of Survey
Schedule patient

Schedule patient.
Service Request

Medical order for health services.
Slot

Agenda spaces available for appointment assignments.
Specimen

A sample to be used for analysis.
Subscription

The subscription resource is used to define a push-based subscription from a server to another system. Once a subscription is registered with the server, the server checks every resource that is created or updated, and if the resource matches the given criteria, it sends a message on the defined “channel” so that another system can take an appropriate action.
Supply delivery

Record of delivery of what is supplied.
Supply request

A record of a request for a medication, substance or device used in the healthcare setting.

Copayment Amount

Subservices

Supplies Amount

VirtualBed

Terminology: Code Systems

These define new code systems used by systems conforming to this implementation guide

CoveragesTypeCl

This is a coding system that contains the types of health coverage applicable to the Chilean jurisdiction.
Nivel Educativo Chile This is a coding system that contains the differents educational level applicable to the Chilean jurisdiction.
ISAPRES

This is a coding system that contains the insurance organizations classified in ISAPRES..
RatesModalityINDISA

This is a coding system that contains the rate modality codes for the interoperability scenario of INDISA (Chile).
TramosFONASA

This is a coding system that contains the the sections (tramos) of FONASA.
